# The Manifesto for Software Generation

The SDLC was built for an era when software was _developed_. That era
is ending.

Software is now _generated_, at AI speed, by humans driving tools that
produce in minutes what once took days. The lifecycle that governs
this work has to be rebuilt around that fact — not bolted onto the
old one.

This is the **Software Generation Life Cycle (SGLC)**. What follows is
its shape: the values it holds, the principles it asks of its
practitioners. Both are stated as laws, not preferences, because they
are intended to outlast any specific tool, methodology, or organisation.

— Ishan De Silva, 2026

---

## Values

**Generated** over developed.

**Drivers** over builders.

**Asked** over assumed.

**Verified** over produced.

**Repo** over chat.

**Captured** over reconstructed.

**Layered** over uniform.

**Fitted** over fixed.

---

## Principles

1. **Decide before generating.** Every artifact begins with someone
   saying what it should be. Make the intent precise before the
   generation runs.

2. **Own the question; release the answer.** The decisions belong to
   humans; the production does not. As tools improve, move further
   upstream, not out of the loop.

3. **Treat the question as the engineering work.** Output quality is
   bounded by the clarity of what was asked. Knowing what to ask, and
   how, is no longer a soft skill.

4. **Make verification keep pace with generation.** When producing is
   cheap, checking is the bottleneck. Staff it accordingly; speed
   without checking is debt.

5. **Put the rules in the repo.** Skills, prompts, principles,
   standards — durable, versioned, owned by name. Guidance that lives
   in chat dies with it.

6. **Capture the reason at the moment of choosing.** The _why_ of a
   generated artifact fades fastest. Record it alongside the work,
   never after.

7. **Layer the specific over the universal.** General principles and
   local rules both apply, additively. Don't replace; extend.

8. **Ship principles; let the implementation fit locally.** No process
   framework survives contact with a real team unaltered. The
   implementation is the team's to shape; the laws are what must survive
   the reshaping.
